Why Modes Matter

1. A mode isn't a scale — it's a perspective. C Ionian and D Dorian use the exact same seven notes (C-D-E-F-G-A-B). The only difference is which note your ear hears as "home." The drone anchors that home — without it, your brain defaults to whatever sounds most like major.

2. The characteristic note is the fingerprint. Every mode has one note that most separates it from its nearest "normal" neighbor. Dorian's raised 6th makes it sound jazzier than natural minor. Lydian's raised 4th makes it float. Learn the characteristic notes and you can name any mode by ear.

3. You already know these sounds. Dorian = "Billie Jean," "So What." Phrygian = flamenco guitar. Lydian = the Simpsons theme, film scores that feel "lifted." Mixolydian = "Sweet Home Alabama," most blues. These aren't exotic — they're the colors you've been hearing your whole life without names.

Try switching rooms while the drone is on. Listen for the moment the vibe shifts — that shift is the mode changing, even though the notes are the same (just starting from a different place).
