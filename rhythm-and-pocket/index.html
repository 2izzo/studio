Glossary

Feel
The rhythmic character of a groove — how the subdivisions are placed in time. Straight, swing, and shuffle are the three families. Same kick and snare, different feel = different song.
Straight
Even 8ths or 16ths. Each subdivision gets equal time. The "on-the-grid" feel of rock, pop, EDM, and most drum machines.
Swing
Uneven 8ths. The "and" of each beat is played late, closer to the next downbeat. Long-short-long-short. Jazz, hip-hop, neo-soul all live here. Tone.Transport.swing.
Shuffle
Triplet 8ths. The beat divides into three equal parts and the hat rides on 1 and 3. DA . da. Blues, Motown, shuffle-rock.
Pocket
Where a drummer's hits sit relative to the click. Ahead = pushed = urgent. Behind = pulled = laid-back. Dead-center = grounded. Usually ±5–25 ms.
Microtiming
Small, intentional deviations from the grid — tens of milliseconds. Invisible on paper, massive to your body. The thing that makes human drummers sound like themselves.
Ghost note
A quiet snare hit between the main ones, usually at 20–30% velocity. Makes the main hits feel louder by contrast and gives the groove a human breath.
Hat density
How many hi-hat hits per beat: quarters = open, 8ths = rock, 16ths = drum & bass / boom-bap. Same pattern, different weather.
J Dilla feel
The unquantized, humanly-nudged drum sound Dilla built by programming each hit by feel instead of on the grid. The DNA of neo-soul and modern hip-hop.
