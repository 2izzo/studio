~ Melody over Chords ~

A chord progression is a field. A melody is a path across it. Some steps land on ground the chord is already holding — those feel stable. Some step outside — those feel like flavor. Some step into thin air — and those are where the magic (or the damage) lives.

The idea

There are three gravitational zones under a given chord. Chord tones are the four notes that chord is literally playing (root, 3rd, 5th, 7th). They feel fused into the harmony. Diatonic tones are the other three notes of the key — they're in the same universe but not in the chord; they create motion. Chromatic tones are the five notes outside the key; they're foreign, and they always want to go somewhere.

The classical rule: land on chord tones on strong beats, use everything else to get between them. The magic rule: break this on purpose. A chromatic passing tone that resolves down a half-step is one of the sweetest sounds in music — used by everyone from Bach to Bill Evans to Beyoncé.

Try the three presets in order. Skeleton is pure chord tones — rock-solid but a little boring. Walk adds diatonic passing tones — it breathes. Chromatic sneaks in a C# and a F# that resolve inwards — suddenly it sounds like a jazz ballad. Same progression, three different melodies, three different ceilings.
