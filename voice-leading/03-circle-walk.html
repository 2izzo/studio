Why greedy works

You don't need to plan ahead. At each chord change, pick the voicing that moves the fewest notes — that's it. Over a 4-bar loop or an 8-chord cycle, the difference between always-root-position and always-nearest can be 20+ semitones of unnecessary motion. Your ear hears that extra motion as "choppy" or "undergrad piano recital." The smooth version sounds like someone who knows what they're doing, even when the actual harmonic content is identical.
