Why this matters

Every chord can be voiced a hundred different ways. Root position, first inversion, second inversion, drop-2, widely spaced, tightly clustered. The cost of a chord change is how far each voice has to travel to get from A to B. Professional voicings minimize that cost — and the smoothness is what makes arrangements sound polished instead of like stacked block chords. The skill is pattern recognition: see the next chord, find the voicing that shares the most notes with the one you just played.
